Peaches

The Prunis persica or peach tree (above) is a decidous tree that stands 13-33 ft. tall. It flowers (right) in spring and the flowers turn into peaches (left).

Peach gelee

Step 1:Line and 8x8 in. baking dish with plastic wrap.

Step 2:In a blender puree 1 lb. of peeled, pitted and sliced peaches and 1 tbsp. of lime juice until vary smooth.

Step 3:Pour into a saucepan over medium heat and add 1/2 cup of sugar and bring to a boil, stirring continuously until thickened which usualy takes about 15 minutes.

Step 4:Stir in another 1 1/2 cups of sugar and 3 tbsp. of liquid pectin. Using a thermometer, heat to 205oF or 96oC and cook, sirring continously for another 10 minutes. Then remove from heat.

Step 5:Pour the finished concoction into the prepared baking dish. Gently shake and tap on flat, sturdy surface then chill for 8+ hours.

Step 6:After removing from fridge let sit until room teperature.

Step 7:Sprinkle about 1/4 cup of sugar on silicone baking mat. Invert the gelee in the mat and remove the plastic wrap and sprinkle about another 1/4 cup of sugar on top.
