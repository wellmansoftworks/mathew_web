Pears

The Pyrus communis or european pear tree (above) which stand usualy about 17 ft. is the most commonly grown and used pear today. They have a bell shape and green to yellow skin, it is considered the traditional pear out west. From april to may they will produce the flowers (right) that turn into pears (left).

Cinnamon baked pears

Step 1: Preheat oven to 400oF then halve pear and scoop out some of there middles.

Step 2: Brush insides of pear with butter and sprinkle 1 tbsp. of cinnamon sugar.

Step 3: In medium mixing bowl add: 1 cup of old fashioned oats, 1/2 cup of finely chopped almonds, 1/3 cups of brown sugar, 1 tsp. of cinnamon and 1/2 cup of melted butter and sprinkle with kosher salt then combine until completely coated.

Step 4: Spoon mixture into pear halves.

Step 5: Bake until pears are soft, usualy takes 35-40 minutes.

Note: It is meant to be served warm.
